# Week 4: Ethics, AI Power and Public Narrative

## Monday: Web3 Law and IP for Creatives Guest Session

Objectives:
- Understand IP ownership
- Learn licensing types
- Understand legal risks
- Learn about regulatory landmines
- Understand cancel culture in Web3
- Learn about personal brand damage
- Understand transparency in building in public

## Deliverable: IP Disclaimer

Add an IP disclaimer to your GitHub repo.

Your disclaimer covers:
- What you claim ownership of
- What others can use
- Licensing terms
- Attribution requirements
- Liability limitations

Add the disclaimer to your "My Web3 Journey" repo README.md or a separate LICENSE file.

## Wednesday: AI for Creatives in Web3 Guest Session

Objectives:
- Master AI as a multiplier for creative productivity and ecosystem contribution
- Learn AI for content ideation, storytelling and DAO moderation
- Learn AI-assisted smart contract explanation and community reporting
- Learn AI-powered Discord bots and creative pipelines
- Learn AI as a co-pilot for brand building in Web3

## Deliverable: AI-Generated Creative Asset

Create an AI-generated creative asset supporting your Web3 roadmap.

Your asset:
- Visualizes your future in Web3
- Uses AI tools to create it
- Supports your roadmap narrative
- Is original and relevant to your goals

Document:
- Tools you used
- Process you followed
- How it supports your roadmap
- Add the asset and documentation to your "My Web3 Journey" repo

## Friday: Building in Public: Crafting Your Web3 Narrative

Objectives:
- Move from learner to future builder
- Understand how public progress builds trust
- Learn that failures and reflections add value
- Learn how to plan a journey: Your Web3 Roadmap

## Deliverable: My Future in Web3 Roadmap

Publish "My future in Web3" roadmap via GitHub and post.

Your roadmap includes:
- Short term goals: next 3 months
- Medium term goals: next 6 to 12 months
- Long term goals: next 1 to 2 years
- Skills you plan to develop
- Projects you want to build
- Communities you want to join
- How you measure success

Publish your roadmap:
1. Add it to your "My Web3 Journey" repo
2. Post it on social media
3. Tag MGSWeb3 and use relevant hashtags

## How to Submit Your Assignment

Follow these steps:

1. Update your fork of this repository
2. Create a new branch for your submission:
   git checkout -b week-4-assignment

3. Add your work inside the submissions folder:
   submissions/
       └── week-4/
           └── your-name/
               ├── ip-disclaimer.md
               ├── ai-creative-asset.md
               └── web3-roadmap.md

4. Include links to your "My Web3 Journey" repo where relevant

5. Include a link to your social media post about your roadmap

6. Commit and push your changes:
   git add .
   git commit -m "chore: submit week 4 assignment"
   git push origin week-4-assignment

7. Create a Pull Request to the main repository. Your PR includes:
   - A brief summary of what you learned
   - Links to your deliverables in your personal repo
   - Link to your roadmap post on social media

8. Post at least one reflection on social media about your Week 4 learnings and your roadmap

## Eviction Checkpoint

You must meet these requirements by end of Week 4:
- Complete all required deliverables for all 4 weeks
- Demonstrate active participation throughout the program
- Publish your Web3 roadmap
- Post roadmap on social media

Incomplete deliverables or lack of participation results in removal.

## Evaluation Criteria

Submissions are evaluated based on:
- Completeness of all three deliverables
- Thoughtfulness of IP disclaimer
- Creativity and relevance of AI-generated asset
- Clarity and ambition of Web3 roadmap
- Consistency in following submission structure
- Public engagement with roadmap post
